    FancyLED

    • Due Oct 4 by 11:59pm
    • Points 10

    Let's get a little practice with Classes, Objects, and Modules.  And while we're at it, let's get a little practice with VS Code.

    In VS Code, make sure your CircuitPython folder is open.  Add a new file called two_fancy.py.  Here is the code:

    from fancyLED import FancyLED

    fancy1 = FancyLED(2,3,4)
    fancy2 = FancyLED(5,6,7)

    while True:
        fancy1.alternate()
        fancy2.blink()
        fancy1.chase()
        fancy2.sparkle()

    Of course, that won't work, right?  None of those lines make sense!

    The assignment: Make a fancyLED.py file.  Inside that, make a FancyLED class so the above code does work.  The FancyLED class takes three numbers.   Those three numbers are pins attached to three LEDs (not RGB LEDs).  So, you'll need 6 LEDs and 6 resistors for this assignment.  Instances of the FancyLED class inherit four methods: alternate, blink, chase, and sparkle.  I'll let you figure out what those four methods should do.
